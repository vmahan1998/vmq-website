Complex Systems Need Simple Models

Transparent, Reproducible, and Co-Developed Modeling Tools

Complex ecological systems don’t need complicated models— they need transparent ones. My goal is to co-develop simple, reproducible modeling tools that reflect community priorities, clarify assumptions, and support real decision-making.

Hydrodynamics

Flow regimes, tidal asymmetry, sediment transport, and estuarine dynamics.

Agent-Based Modeling

Behavior, migration, schooling, cues, and exposure pathways.

Habitat Modeling

Life-stage HSI, integrated fitness, and Penobscot River mapping.

Co-Development & Outreach

Workshops, partnerships, mentoring, and science communication.

Five Pillars of My Modeling Philosophy

Transparent Modeling

I build models that clearly state their assumptions, uncertainty, and limitations, replacing black-box tools with transparent, communicable frameworks grounded in ecological realism.

Reproducible Science

From hydrodynamic preprocessing to agent-based simulations, every workflow is version-controlled, documented, and openly accessible to support reproducibility and scientific trust.

Co-Development

I collaborate with Tribal partners, federal agencies, fisheries managers, and local communities to ensure model assumptions and outputs reflect lived experience and shared priorities.

Simplicity in Complexity

Complex systems don’t need complicated tools, but models that isolate key mechanisms and stay adaptable and accessible.

Decision Support

I design tools that support decision-making by visualizing risk, mapping habitat, exploring scenarios, and helping communities and agencies respond to environmental challenges.

Who Am I?

I’m a PhD candidate studying hydrodynamics, mercury contamination, and anadromous fish ecology in the Penobscot River. My work blends community engagement, modeling, and science communication.

Boost Your Coding Skills

Learn the tools I use every day—from R and Python to NetLogo, MATLAB, and Version Control—through clear, accessible lessons rooted in real ecological modeling workflows.

Modeling Toolkit & Library

Explore my open-source modeling toolkit, including hydrodynamic preprocessing, habitat modeling, ABM functions, groundwater–surface water workflows, and reproducible research templates.
